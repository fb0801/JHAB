Advantages and Disadvantages of Content Managemt Systems (CMS)?

Dolapo 2019

Advantages of Content Management Systems

  1. It enables on to add and edit pages on a website. Forinstance to add images, videos
  2. Low Cost; it is cost effective, because it eliminates payments to Developer for a monthly maintainance fees.
  3. Ability to delegate roles and pass this through out the organisatio, there's room for division of labour, where people will be involved in writing of the contents, and someone else can verify the contents.
  4. The use of CMS enables the developer to leave content management to the client , which in turn free him from content management.
  5. The use of CMS facilitates faster website development.
  6. The use of CMS enables the developer to build in SEO and friendly URLs.
  7. The use of CMS gives developers the ability to utilize good third party plugins.

Disdvantages of Content Management Systems

  1. The use of CMS induces overhead costs on server resources.
  2. The use of CMS often calls for additional maintenance, backups, upgrades and security patches.
  3. The misuse of CMS by clients can damage the structure and image of a website, and by on implications the image of the developer.
  4. The use of CMS calls for additional training which can inflate the cost of the project.
  5. The use of CMS by clients can create room for faulty text design.
  6. The misuse of a CMS has the ability to damage the look and feel of your website.
  7. Clients often lack the trained and motivated staff who can carry out regular updates on their website.
